The Industries Data Scientists work in?

The first thing which goes into your mind when talking about the workplace of a data scientist, would be Google of Apple. And you will most probably be right! But what if I told you data scientists are essential in organisations such as the Australian Metereology Department and even our own universities.

As expected Information technology companies or "Tech Companies" have almost twice the amount of job listings for data scientists compared to the science and technology market. If you wish to pursue a career as a data scientist, the best states to move to are Victoria and New South Wales which have more than 1000 job listings in the past three years. In comparison, Northern Territory and Tasmania is particularly redundant in the requirement for data scientists, particularly in the last three years.



In the banking and financial services industry, the highest amount of jobs advertised are placed in the analysis and reporting sector. This industry is the most niche since it has a lower quantity of jobs advertised when compared to the other two industries. It also has the least variety of classifications. The ICT industry has the highest number of job listings and there are various classifications in which these jobs are advertised in, ranging from developers to quality assurance and control. There is also a predominant amount of job listings in the science and technology industry, where the main area of interest resides in mathematics, statistics and information, where around 857 jobs are advertised. This industry has a moderate amount of classifications, which are around 12 in number.

Sub Classifications compare?



It can be perceived that the job listings advertised for data scientists are mainly composed of full time roles, with a percentage of more than 75%. In contrast, casual and part time work types make up a significantly low percentage of the jobs advertised. The contract/ temporary work type, accounts for around 20% of the job listings for data scientists

Though in the recent years there's a significant rise of vacationer and casual job listings. This is a good sign since it allows students to dip themselves into the depths of data science without working as a permenent employee.

Did the Pandemic change anything?

It can be observed that the aggregate of job listings for data scientists are varying from the years 2019 to 2021. In 2019, there was a high proportion of jobs advertised, particularly in the months on March and May and there is a steady decrease in the latter half of the year. 2020 has had a significant impact on the job market for data scientists since there is notable decrease in job listings compared to 2019 and 2021 in most of the months. In 2021, there is an increment in the jobs advertised for data scientists and a greater peak in the month of May compared to 2019. However, it can be seen that there is dwindle after the month of May.
